Welcome to the AI Learning Journal

Your personal reference for understanding AI — not just what things are, but how they work together. Here is how to get the most out of it in under 3 minutes.

Step 1
Search or browse the Term Library

Every AI concept, tool, and technology has a card with three sections: Plain English (what it is in simple terms), How it actually works (the real mechanism), and a Real-world example grounded in things being built today.

Use the search bar to find any term instantly, or filter by category using the colored pills — AI Assistants, Core AI Concepts, Developer Tools, and more.

Step 2
Follow a Learning Path for your goal

Learning Paths are curated sequences of terms organized around a goal. Technical tracks cover how AI works and how to build with it. Creative tracks cover image, content, and video creation. Functional tracks cover specific business roles like Marketing, Sales, and Operations.

Click a path card to activate it — the term grid reorganizes into that path's recommended sequence, with Previous and Next buttons so you move through them one at a time.

Pro tip: Combine a path with a category filter. Select the AI to Build path then click Business & Work Tools to see only the business tools relevant to builders.

Step 3
Study Case Studies to see it all in action

Case Studies show real scenarios — the Telegram bot you already built, a morning AI routine, an email triage system, a video production workflow. Each one shows the complete flow diagram of what is happening technically, then lists every term involved in the order you encounter them while building it.

Click any term card inside a case study to expand it and read the full definition without leaving the page.

Step 4
Track your progress as you learn

Every term card has a Mark as Understood button. When you click it, the button turns green and a small green dot appears on the card. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser and persists across sessions.

The progress bar at the top of the page tracks how many of the 99 concepts you have marked. When following a Learning Path, the path banner also shows your completion progress for that specific track.

Note: Progress is stored in your browser's local storage. It stays even when you close the tab, but resets if you clear your browser data.
