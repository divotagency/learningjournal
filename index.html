◆ Why this matters
AI Fluency is not about knowing which buttons to click. It is about developing the judgment to use AI well — with any tool, in any situation. These slides give you the concepts and the prompts to start getting real value from AI on your very next session.
◆ Foundations  ·  Slide 2 of 13
What is Generative AI?
AI systems that create new content — not just classify or analyze existing data. The key shift: from AI that categorizes to AI that generates.
📊 Traditional AI
Classifies emails as spam or not spam
Detects fraud in a transaction
Recommends a product based on history
Analyzes sentiment in existing reviews
→ Works within defined categories
✨ Generative AI
Writes a completely new email for you
Explains why something looks suspicious
Creates a product description from scratch
Drafts a response to a negative review
→ Creates new content from patterns
◆ The core idea
Generative AI learned patterns from billions of text examples. It can now predict what should come next — a word, a sentence, a piece of code — with enough accuracy to be genuinely useful for real-world tasks.
◆ Foundations  ·  Slide 3 of 13
Three Pillars That Made It Possible
Three converging breakthroughs — not one. Generative AI required algorithms, data, and compute to all reach sufficient scale at the same time.
⚙
Algorithms
Transformer architecture (2017) revolutionized how models process long text passages. Attention mechanisms let models relate any word to any other word in a sequence — no matter the distance.
📊
Data Explosion
Trillions of tokens from websites, books, code repositories, and text sources provided the raw material to train these systems at meaningful scale.
🔥
Computing Power
Massive GPU advances made it economically feasible to train models on all that data — what would have taken decades on 2010 hardware now runs in weeks.
◆ Why 2017–2023 specifically?
All three pillars crossed their threshold simultaneously. The transformer paper dropped in 2017. GPT-2 (2019) showed the scale potential. GPT-3 (2020) proved practical usefulness. ChatGPT (2022) put it in everyone's hands. Each relied on the others.
◆ Foundations  ·  Slide 4 of 13
How It Works — 3 Stages
Every major AI model goes through the same three-stage journey from raw data to useful assistant.
1
Pre-training
The model analyzes billions of text examples and learns to predict what word comes next — billions of times. This is where the model builds its broad understanding of language, facts, and reasoning patterns. It is expensive, slow, and done once by the AI company.
2
Fine-tuning
The pre-trained model is refined to follow instructions, be helpful, and avoid harmful content. Human raters score responses, and the model learns from their preferences (RLHF). This is what turns a raw language predictor into a useful assistant.
3
Deployment
Users provide prompts, and the model generates responses based on the prompts and the patterns it learned during training. Every response is generated fresh — token by token — from learned probability distributions, not retrieved from a database.

◆ Capabilities  ·  Slide 5 of 13
What AI Assistants Can Do For You
Modern AI assistants (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and others) share the same core capabilities. These work the same regardless of which tool you use.
✎
Write & Edit
Drafts, edits, rewrites, summarizes, and reformats content across styles, tones, and audiences. Give it your rough draft and ask it to improve it.
💬
Think With You
Brainstorm, pressure-test your ideas, play devil’s advocate, or help you see a problem from a new angle. It does not get tired or judgmental.
🔎
Research & Summarize
Synthesizes long documents, identifies patterns, compares options. Paste in a report and ask “what are the three most important points?”
💻
Explain Anything
Translate jargon into plain language, explain technical concepts at any level, or describe how something works step by step.
⚡
Automate Repetition
Reformat data, generate variations of a template, classify a list of items, convert between formats. If it follows a pattern, AI can repeat it.
📱
Work With Your Files
Paste text, upload PDFs, images, or spreadsheets and AI works from your actual content — not assumptions about what you might have.
◆ Try this right now
Open any AI assistant and paste this: “I have a task I do at work regularly: [describe it in one sentence]. What are three ways AI could help me do this faster or better?” — you will have a starting point within 30 seconds.
◆ Action  ·  Slide 6 of 13
Write a Great Prompt — Right Now
The difference between a mediocre and a genuinely useful AI response is almost always the quality of the prompt. Here are four ready-to-use templates — copy, fill in the brackets, send.
✎
For writing or editing
“I need to write a [email / report / message] to [audience]. The goal is to [desired outcome]. The tone should be [formal / casual / direct]. Here is my rough draft: [paste draft]. Please improve it.”
🔎
For summarizing or analyzing
“Here is a [document / article / email thread]: [paste content]. Summarize the key points in 3 bullet points. Then tell me the one thing I most need to act on.”
💬
For brainstorming or deciding
“I am trying to decide between [option A] and [option B] for [situation]. I care most about [criteria]. Give me the honest pros and cons of each, then your recommendation.”
⚡
For explaining something
“Explain [concept or term] to me as if I am [a complete beginner / a busy professional / a 10-year-old]. Use an analogy I can relate to. Keep it under 150 words.”
◆ The pattern behind every template
Role or situation (who you are / what this is for)  +  Clear action verb (summarize, write, compare, explain)  +  Your actual content (pasted in)  +  One constraint (tone, length, format). That four-part structure outperforms vague questions every time.
◆ AI Fluency  ·  Slide 7 of 13
What is AI Fluency?
AI Fluency is the ability to collaborate effectively with AI tools — not just knowing which buttons to click, but developing the judgment to use AI well across different situations.
◆ The 4D Framework — Anthropic
Four interconnected competencies ensure your interactions with AI are effective, efficient, ethical, and safe. These are not sequential steps — they are ongoing mindsets that work together.
🎯 Delegation
Setting goals & deciding when/how to engage
Deciding what work should be done by humans, what by AI, and how to distribute tasks between them. Includes understanding your goals, AI capabilities, and making strategic choices about collaboration.
💬 Description
Effectively communicating goals to AI
Effectively communicating with AI systems. Includes clearly defining outputs, guiding AI processes, and specifying desired AI behaviors and interactions.
🔍 Discernment
Accurately assessing AI outputs
Thoughtfully and critically evaluating AI outputs, processes, behaviors, and interactions. Includes assessing quality, accuracy, appropriateness, and determining areas for improvement.
⚔ Diligence
Taking responsibility for AI-assisted work
Using AI responsibly and ethically. Includes making thoughtful choices about AI systems, maintaining transparency, and taking accountability for AI-assisted work.
◆ AI Fluency  ·  Slide 8 of 13
Three Modes of AI Interaction
Not all AI interactions are the same. Knowing which mode you are in — and choosing it deliberately — is a core Delegation skill.
⚡
Automation
AI executes — you direct
AI executes specific tasks based on human instructions. You define the task precisely; the AI performs it. Highest efficiency when the task is well-defined and repeatable.
Email draftingSummarizationClassification
🤝
Augmentation
Humans + AI think together
Humans and AI collaborate as thinking partners. You bring judgment, context, and goals; AI brings processing speed, breadth of knowledge, and tireless iteration. Best for complex, open-ended work.
StrategyResearchBrainstorming
🤖
Agency
AI acts independently
Humans configure AI to independently perform future tasks on their behalf. The AI makes decisions and takes actions without real-time human input. Requires careful setup and monitoring.
AgentsWorkflowsAutomation
◆ Delegation in practice
Most people start in Automation mode and gradually move toward Augmentation as they develop AI Fluency. Agency mode requires the most skill and care — it is where the highest leverage and the highest risk both live.
◆ Description  ·  Slide 9 of 13
The Prompting Framework
Description is the skill of communicating with AI clearly. Anthropic’s three-part structure — Stage, Task, Rules — captures what every good prompt needs.
1
🏁 Setting the Stage
What is your role and what are your objectives? Give the AI the context it needs to calibrate. Who are you, what organization or project is this for, and what background matters here? The more relevant context you provide, the less the AI has to assume.
2
✎ Defining the Task
What action do you want the AI to take? Be specific about the verb: write, analyze, build, compare, summarize, rewrite, critique. Vague instructions produce vague outputs. The clearer the action, the more targeted the result.
3
🎯 Specifying Rules
What constraints, style, or format apply? Tone (formal/casual), length (under 200 words), format (bullet points, table, paragraph), audience (executive/technical), and examples of what good looks like. Rules are what transform a generic response into a useful one.
◆ Try this on your next prompt
Stage: “I am a [your role] working on [project type].”   Task: “Write / Analyze / Compare / Explain [specific thing].”   Rules: “Use a [tone] tone, keep it under [length], format as [bullets/paragraphs/table].” — Any AI assistant responds better when you fill in all three.
◆ Discernment  ·  Slide 10 of 13
Iterating & Evaluating Responses
Getting a good response is rarely a one-shot event. Discernment means knowing how to assess what you got and knowing the right move next.
🔍
Ask Follow-ups
“Can you expand on the second point?” or “Make it more concise.” Build on what worked.
💬
Give Feedback
“This is good but the tone is too formal — make it more conversational.” Be specific about what to change.
🔄
Redirect
“Actually, I was asking about X, not Y. Let me clarify...” Steer it back without starting over.
🔄
Restart
If the conversation has drifted too far, start a fresh chat to fully reset the context. Sometimes cleaner than recovering.
◆ What to look for
Does it capture the key information? Is the tone and style appropriate? What is missing or could be improved? Are any facts verifiable?
◆ Try this on your next prompt
After any response: type “Good start. Now make it [shorter / more direct / less formal / more specific about X].” One follow-up message transforms an okay response into a useful one more reliably than rewriting the whole prompt from scratch.
◆ Diligence  ·  Slide 11 of 13
Using AI Responsibly
Diligence means taking accountability for AI-assisted work — not just using AI, but using it in ways that are transparent, ethical, and safe.
⚔
Take Accountability
You are responsible for AI-assisted outputs. If AI writes something you send, you are still the author. Review, verify, and own what goes out under your name.
👁
Maintain Transparency
Be honest about when and how AI was used — especially in professional, academic, or high-stakes contexts where disclosure matters.
🔒
Protect Privacy
Be thoughtful about what data you share with AI systems — especially sensitive personal, financial, or confidential business information.
◆ The hallucination risk
AI models can confidently produce plausible-sounding but incorrect information. Always verify factual claims, especially specific names, dates, statistics, legal details, and citations. Never publish AI-generated facts without checking them.
◆ Practical Skills  ·  Slide 12 of 13
Setting Up Evals
Evals are a practical approach to understanding how AI performs for your specific workflows — replacing guesswork with structured testing before you fully rely on AI for a task.
1
Gather Examples
Collect 5–10 examples of a task you do regularly — emails you have written, reports you have created, analyses you have done. These become your quality benchmark.
2
Create Test Prompts
Write prompts that would generate similar outputs to your examples. Include the context you would naturally have when doing this work.
3
Compare Outputs
Run your prompts and compare the AI’s responses to your examples. Ask: does it capture the key information? Is the tone appropriate? What is missing?
4
Refine Your Approach
Based on what you learn: adjust prompts, add examples to show the AI what good looks like, or identify where human review is essential before acting on AI outputs.
◆ Summary  ·  Slide 13 of 13
Key Takeaways
The most important ideas from the AI Fluency curriculum — worth returning to whenever you are figuring out how to use AI more effectively.
1
AI generates, it does not retrieve
Responses are created token by token from learned patterns — not looked up. This is why hallucination happens and why RAG and verification matter.
2
Context is everything
AI has no memory between conversations. Each session starts fresh. The quality of your output is directly proportional to the quality of context you provide in each prompt.
3
Stage, Task, Rules
Every good prompt answers three questions: who you are and why, what you want the AI to do, and what constraints apply to the output.
4
Fluency = judgment, not clicks
AI Fluency develops through practice. The 4Ds — Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence — are skills that compound over time.
5
You are still accountable
AI-assisted outputs are still your outputs. Verify before you publish, disclose when it matters, and never outsource your judgment to a model.
6
Iterate, don’t give up
The first response is rarely the best one. Build a habit of follow-up, feedback, and refinement — that is where the real leverage is.
